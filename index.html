01 — Why this exists

Reproductive health education has gaps.
We're trying to close them.

Our research and interviews with students kept surfacing the same story: health class covers the basics of a period, then stops. Conditions like endometriosis and PCOS go unnamed for years — and the students most affected are often the ones least believed.

◐

Inconsistent

What you learn about your own body depends on your school, your teacher, and whether the topic gets skipped for time.

◑

Incomplete

Many students say they never learned to tell "normal" period pain from symptoms that deserve a doctor's visit.

◒

Not inclusive

Race, gender, and age all shape whether pain gets taken seriously — in the classroom and in the exam room.
