01 — Breaking the silence

Why this website exists.

For many young people, conversations about reproductive health are limited, uncomfortable, or overlooked. Symptoms like severe period pain, heavy bleeding, chronic fatigue, or irregular cycles are often dismissed as "normal," delaying diagnosis for conditions like endometriosis, PMOS, adenomyosis, and uterine fibroids. Inspired by youth-led research and lived experience, this platform exists so every young person feels informed, supported, and empowered to seek help when something doesn't feel right.

◐

Inconsistent

What you learn about your own body depends on your school, your teacher, and whether the topic gets skipped for time.

◑

Incomplete

Many students say they never learned to tell "normal" period pain from symptoms that deserve a doctor's visit.

◒

Not inclusive

Race, gender, and age all shape whether pain gets taken seriously — in the classroom and in the exam room.

06 — Meet the researcher

About Elyse.

Hi, I'm Elyse Fisher-Uriarte. I created this website as part of a Youth Participatory Action Research (YPAR) project through the Toronto District School Board's Black Student Summer Leadership Program.

Living with endometriosis inspired me to explore why reproductive illnesses are often misunderstood, under-researched, and overlooked — especially among teenagers and racialized communities. Through research, anonymous testimonies, and educational resource development, I hope this platform helps young people feel seen, informed, and empowered to advocate for their health.
